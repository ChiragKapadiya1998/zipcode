Trail 4: The Elysian Staircase
Vibe: Hidden gems + visual discovery | H-Facet: H5 (Shareability → organic CAC) |
Beachhead: H0A primary, H0B secondary Duration: ~2 hours | Budget: $0–20/person (mostly
walking, optional coffee at base)
Hypothesis: Elysian Heights&#39; hidden stairways, elevated murals, and city-view moments

Stop 1 — Baxter Street (base), Baxter St at Avon St | 10 min Why: The steepest public street
in Los Angeles (33% grade). Most LA residents have heard of it; almost none have visited.
Insider tip: Stand at the base and look up — you don&#39;t have to climb it. The trail goes up a
different route. Reveal: &quot;At 33% grade, this is the steepest street in Los Angeles. Most people
who live nearby know it exists. Almost no one comes here. In winter, cars get stuck on the
incline. The neighborhood association tried to close it twice. Both failed. The next stop is the
reason it stays open — there&#39;s something worth seeing at the top.&quot;
Stop 2 — Elysian Heights Stairs (Kenilworth Ave set) | 30 min Why: A network of public
concrete stairways cut through the hillside — some unnamed, partially collapsed, all technically
accessible. Not marked on Google Maps. Insider tip: Bring water. Explore 2–3 sets of stairs.
Position yourself on the Kenilworth stairway landing facing northwest. Reveal: &quot;These stairs
were built for hillside residents before cars were common in the 1920s. They&#39;ve never been
removed. There are at least 12 sets in Elysian Heights. The view across to Silver Lake
Reservoir and DTLA from the Kenilworth landing is a shot almost no one in LA has taken. Post
it. Tell them where it is.&quot;
Stop 3 (PEAK — visual) — Elysian Heights mural corridor, upper Alvarado/Baxter turn |
35 min Why: Community murals visible from specific stairway landings — smaller pieces that
require being on the stairs to see. Peak for H5: unique enough that location curiosity drives
social engagement. Insider tip: The mural on the upper Alvarado turn is only visible from the
landing at the top of the second Kenilworth stairway. Face it directly at noon. Reveal: &quot;This has
been here since 2017. It&#39;s designed to be photographed from exactly this spot. Tag @cityunlock
to unlock a bonus stop on the next trail in this neighborhood.&quot;
Stop 4 (Resolution) — Elysian Park overlook | 25 min Why: A quiet overlook in the oldest
municipal park in LA — accessible in under 10 minutes from the mural corridor. View of Dodger
Stadium, DTLA, and San Gabriel Mountains. Insider tip: Early morning or late afternoon for
clearest air. Reveal: &quot;Elysian Park opened in 1886. It&#39;s older than most of the neighborhoods
you walked through to get here. This overlook faces the same city those neighborhoods grew up

around. You&#39;ve been looking at it from the hillside for two hours. Now you&#39;re looking out from the
top of it.&quot;
Experience Layer
- Hook (Stop 1): &quot;Most people who live in LA have heard of Baxter Street. Today you&#39;re
actually here.&quot; Shareability from the first unlock.
- Deepen (Stop 2): Stairway network is genuinely unknown — generates &quot;wait WHERE is
this??&quot; reactions.
- Peak (Stop 3): Mural only reachable from the stairway landing. Location uniqueness
drives organic posts.
- Payoff (Stop 4): Park overlook contextualizes everything below — city-wide view earns

H5 Signal Guide
- Go: 40%+ of completers post content organically; UTM shows &gt;25% of new LP visitors
from social; at least one stop generates unprompted location questions
- No-go: &lt;15% organic post rate; all acquisition remains paid/direct
- Critical dependency: UTM tracking must be live before pilot launch — not retrofittable.

Trail 5: York Boulevard, Floor One
Vibe: Neighborhood unlock | H-Facet: H6 (Repeat intent) | Beachhead: H0A primary Duration:
~2 hours | Budget: $15–25/person at stops (coffee + drinks)
Hypothesis: A trail that explicitly ends with &quot;there&#39;s a second trail in this neighborhood&quot;

Stop 1 — Kitchen Mouse, 5904 N Figueroa St | 20 min Why: The kind of café that long-term

south from here. Reveal: &quot;This is where the York corridor begins — not at the busy part, but four

Stop 2 — Permanent Records, 5116 York Blvd | 30 min Why: An independent record store
that&#39;s survived gentrification by becoming more specific, not broader. Known for carrying

the &quot;LA local only&quot; bin near the counter. The staff knows who pressed every record in it. Reveal:
&quot;Everything that opened around this store in 2012–2019 has cycled once or twice. This store

Stop 3 (PEAK) — York Blvd mural corridor, Ave 55–57 block | 30 min Why: A mural

A Highland Park bar and live music venue, in the neighborhood for over a decade. Shows local
