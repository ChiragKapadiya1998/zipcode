Trail 1: The Figueroa Frequency
Vibe: Neighborhood deep-dive | H-Facet: H1 (WTP) | Beachhead: H0A primary, H0B
secondary Duration: ~2 hours | Budget: $15–25/person at stops (coffee + food)
Hypothesis: A deeply local, non-Instagram-famous Highland Park trail justifies $20–30/person
because the insider access is the product — not just the stops. Less-discovered neighborhood
= stronger insider premium = clearest WTP test condition.

Stop 1 — Kumquat Coffee, 5520 N Figueroa St | 20 min Why: One of the last pre-
gentrification Figueroa anchors. The 2009 corner build-out is still original. Entry point that meets
the neighborhood on its own terms. Insider tip: Order the house blend. Say you&#39;re walking the
trail — the baristas know where you&#39;re headed. Reveal: &quot;This block looked the same in 2009
when the neighborhood was mostly uncharted. You&#39;re starting where the trail begins — at a
business that chose to stay. The next stop is four blocks south and about forty years deeper.&quot;
Stop 2 — Figueroa Street Murals, Ave 53 block | 25 min Why: A mural corridor between Ave
53 and Ave 57 — one of the oldest publicly-funded Chicano mural programs in LA. Most
residents have driven past without stopping. Insider tip: Walk the full block; read the wall. Find
the caption tiles at the base of each panel. Reveal: &quot;Before Figueroa became a coffee
destination, this wall was the neighborhood&#39;s newspaper. The faces here are real people —
names in the caption tiles at the base. Find the one titled &#39;1947.&#39; That&#39;s the oldest one still
standing.&quot;
Stop 3 (PEAK) — Avenue 50 Studio, 131 N Avenue 50 | 35 min Why: Community arts gallery
open to walk-ins, running continuously since 1994. Rotating shows by Northeast LA artists. The
building is unmarked from the street — not findable without knowing to look. Insider tip: The
front gate looks locked. It&#39;s not — push it. The studio is in the back courtyard. Reveal:
&quot;Whoever&#39;s there will show you the current exhibition and they know the building&#39;s history. This
is the room where many of the mural artists above were first shown. Nobody outside the
neighborhood knows this place exists.&quot;
Stop 4 (Resolution) — Highland Park Bowl, 5621 N Figueroa St | 30–40 min Why: A 1927
Prohibition-era bowling alley with original pin-setting mechanisms intact, serving pizza and craft
beer. Discovered by a small community of regulars who kept it alive before its 2016 restoration.
Insider tip: Order a pizza, ask about the lane history. The staff knows the whole arc. Reveal:
&quot;This is the payoff. You&#39;ve walked the full arc — from a corner that survived gentrification,
through a wall that documented what this neighborhood was, to an arts room that still operates
the same way, to a 1927 bowling alley with original machinery. The city has layers. You just
walked through three of them.&quot;

Experience Layer
- Hook (Stop 1): &quot;You live 20 minutes from this and have never been.&quot; First unlock
signals depth, not tourism.
- Deepen (Stop 2): Mural corridor reframes neighborhood identity — history is physically
present on these walls.
- Peak (Stop 3): Avenue 50 Studio is the reveal that earns the price — an unlockable
place behind an unmarked gate.
- Payoff (Stop 4): Highland Park Bowl is earned celebration. The trail made this place
mean something it wouldn&#39;t have without context.
H1 Signal Guide
- Go: LP conversion &gt;3%; 100+ trail accesses in 6 weeks; post-trail top response =
&quot;couldn&#39;t have built this myself&quot;
- No-go: Sub-2% conversion; users say &quot;I could have Googled this&quot; (no insider premium
perceived)
- Watch for: H0A needs to feel the trail compensated for planning overhead. If WTP is
low, check whether reveal descriptions made stops feel sufficiently exclusive.

Trail 2: The Silver Lake Social Circuit
Vibe: Local ritual | H-Facet: H2 (Group &gt; solo) | Beachhead: H0A primary Duration: ~2.5
hours | Budget: $25–45/person at stops (coffee + food + drinks)
Hypothesis: Silver Lake&#39;s density of high-social-value stops makes this trail significantly better
with 2–4 people than solo. Group comparison produces H2 signal only if the trail is explicitly
designed for shared discovery and communal debrief.

Stop 1 — Intelligentsia Coffee, 3922 W Sunset Blvd | 20 min Why: Not undiscovered — that&#39;s
the point. The neighborhood&#39;s social rituals begin here. Starting here signals: this trail runs on
group energy. Insider tip: Don&#39;t linger — this is activation, not destination. Reveal: &quot;Every person
around you is about to go somewhere specific in Silver Lake. The difference: you&#39;re about to
actually go. The next stop is 7 minutes on foot — and most people who walk past it daily don&#39;t
know it&#39;s open to the public.&quot;
Stop 2 — Silver Lake Conservatory of Music, 4001 Sunset Blvd | 25 min Why: Founded by
Flea (RHCP), community-funded, free concerts on rotating Saturdays. The lobby is open to
walk-ins. Most people associate it only with music lessons. Insider tip: Walk in. Check the
bulletin board. If someone is rehearsing, you&#39;re allowed to listen. Reveal: &quot;This is Flea&#39;s building
— not the Flea who toured stadiums, the Flea who grew up in Silver Lake and put a community
music school here. Walk in. They always have someone practicing. If you&#39;re with someone,
you&#39;ll both stop talking.&quot;
Stop 3 (PEAK — time-sensitive) — Silver Lake Reservoir, Rowena Ave entrance | 40 min
Why: The reservoir lap (Rowena Ave to Silver Lake Blvd, 1.3 miles) is one of the best urban
walks in LA. Peak signal: arrive between 5–6 PM when the water catches the western sky.
Insider tip: Take Rowena Ave, not the Silver Lake Blvd entrance. At the midpoint, face
southwest at 5:30 PM. Reveal: &quot;The east side entrance on Silver Lake Blvd is where everyone
goes. The far side at Rowena Ave is where you go. At the midpoint, face southwest at 5:30 PM.
The reservoir turns completely gold. You cannot recreate this in a photograph. Bring your group
— this is the moment this trail is designed for.&quot;
Stop 4 (Resolution — communal) — Bar Flores, 3804 Sunset Blvd | 40+ min Why: Small
natural wine bar built around group debrief culture — long tables, shared plates, no screens.
Walk-in friendly before 7 PM. Insider tip: Ask for the communal table. Order one bottle for the
group. Reveal: &quot;Order one bottle between your group. You&#39;ve just walked 2+ miles of a
neighborhood you probably drove through a hundred times. This is where you figure out what
you actually think about it.&quot;
